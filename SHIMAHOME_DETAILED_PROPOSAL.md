## CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

### 1.0 Introduction
In full details, this section introduces the ShimaHome project, providing a comprehensive overview of the rental property management system being developed. The introduction sets the stage for understanding the context, significance, and scope of the project within the Kenyan real estate sector.

### 1.1 Background to the Study
The Kenyan real estate sector has undergone significant transformation over the past two decades, emerging as one of the most dynamic sectors in the country's economy. This section provides a comprehensive analysis of the property management landscape in Kenya, focusing on the rental housing market, which constitutes approximately 80% of the urban housing stock according to the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) 2022 report.

#### 1.1.1 Historical Context of Property Management in Kenya
Property management in Kenya has evolved from traditional, informal arrangements to more structured systems. The journey can be categorized into distinct phases:

1. **Pre-Independence Era (Before 1963)**
   - Predominantly colonial administration of properties
   - Segregated housing policies
   - Limited property ownership rights for locals

2. **Post-Independence Period (1963-1990)**
   - Emergence of local property owners
   - Development of housing cooperatives
   - Introduction of basic property management practices

3. **Liberalization Era (1990-2010)**
   - Market liberalization and increased foreign investment
   - Growth of property management companies
   - Introduction of basic digital record-keeping

4. **Digital Age (2010-Present)**
   - Adoption of property management software
   - Mobile money integration
   - Emergence of proptech startups

#### 1.1.2 Current Market Overview

##### 1.1.2.1 Market Size and Growth
- The Kenyan real estate sector contributes approximately 7.2% to the national GDP (KNBS 2023)
- Rental yields average between 5-8% in major urban centers
- Nairobi's rental market is valued at KES 120 billion annually
- 60% of urban households live in rental accommodation (World Bank 2023)

##### 1.1.2.2 Key Market Segments
1. **Residential Rental Market**
   - High-end apartments
   - Middle-income housing
   - Low-cost housing
   - Student hostels
   - Serviced apartments

2. **Commercial Rental Market**
   - Office spaces
   - Retail spaces
   - Industrial properties
   - Warehousing
